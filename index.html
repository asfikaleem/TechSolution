About Us

Who We Are

  • For parents who want a balanced approach to educating their child in mind, body, and spirit, the Tech Solution of Oshawa provides a challenging, Six dimension of wellness curriculum in a healthy, joyful, and screen-free environment.
  • One of over 1200, Tech Solution Institution, TS was founded in 2001 and serves 180 students from preschool through grade 8. Our program features highly trained, experienced faculty and an academic curriculum that is carefully mapped to children’s cognitive, emotional, and physical developmental stages.
  • Beginning in first grade, students study math, science, the humanities, and one native languages (French). Music and the visual arts enrich each school day across the grades, and all students act in a class play and learn traditional handwork skills.
  • Athletics and movement are an important part of every student’s day. Students spend time outdoors, exploring the natural world in nearby conservation land. Students also participate in gardening program, farm and field trips, and seasonal festivals.
  • At the Tech Solution of Oshawa, we prepare students for a lifetime of engaged, self-directed learning. Above all, TS is a after school institution where each child is seen, known, and loved. And that can change the world.

An Education for the Future

Our community nurtures ethical, active, and impactful world citizens who thrive independently and collaboratively throughout their lives. By integrating creativity and beauty into every aspect of our curriculum and school culture, our students discover their unique connection to learning.

Faculty and Staff

  • A fundamental tenet of Tech education is that the loving guidance of a class teacher, whom children know and trust, helps them to grow into self-confident learners and adults. We are very proud of the fact that the Tech Solution Institute of Oshawa is a place where every child is seen, known, and loved.
  • Tech class teachers are authorities on their subject matter, as well as storytellers, musicians, artists, and actors. They teach the first one-hour lesson block of the day covering primarily Olympiad, Reasoning, Mental math, and Physical activity. The class teacher is also responsible for students’ overall academic and social development. Subject specialists in world languages, math, reading, English, music, movement, handwork, woodwork, and athletics teach students throughout the grades.
  • All Tech teachers are highly trained, credentialed, and experienced. Class teachers in each grade hold both a college degree and teacher certification from one of several recognized teacher training colleges or institutes.

Program

Explore Grades 1–5

  • During the elementary years, a student’s imagination and feelings serve as the gateway to deep, sustained learning and intellectual growth. Tech teachers present academic material artistically through drama, story, art, music, and movement. This interdisciplinary approach engenders a deep love of learning and challenges students on multiple levels.
  • Expectations and student accountability increase in the middle school years. Teachers strive to ensure students are well-prepared for the rigors of high school. Fourth and fifth grade students receive additional classes in mathematics and Science skills; they are challenged to manage long-term and independent projects; and they deepen their understanding of important concepts.

    • Main Lesson
  • At the heart of the Tech Solution curriculum is the “Main Lesson”, a one-hour period of concentrated inquiry that begins each school day, when students are freshest and ready for sustained effort. In Main Lesson, we study one of four central subjects for a “block” of three to four weeks, inviting in-depth exploration and learning. Our four central subjects are:
  • Physical activity
  • Olympiad
  • Mental Math
  • Reasoning

Class Teacher
  • Every morning, as each child crosses the threshold for Main Lesson, he or she is welcomed by the Class Teacher with a handshake. Ideally, this individual leads the students from first through fifth grades, for five years of handshakes and Main Lessons. The Class Teacher serves as an important role model for students, nurturing everyone’s potential in the context of a strong relationship. Because social learning is central to Tech Solution education, the class teacher acts as a guide for the class, fostering cooperation and mutual respect.

    Our Screen-Free Approach


    We are often asked why we don’t use computers for instruction at TS, and why we recommend a screen-free life for our students at home, especially in the early childhood and elementary years.

    Technology can be a wonderful tool. However, the promise of technology in education has fallen far short of its aims—while study after study warns of the harm technology and media use pose for children, particularly during the early years of critical brain development.

    At the Tech Solution of Oshawa, our classrooms are screen-free, and our students thrive, both academically and socially. Our “unplugged” approach is backed not only by current research, but by the hundreds of thoughtful, creative, successful students who have graduated from the Tech Solution Institution of Oshawa.

    Festivals & Special Events


    Along with the school's academic curriculum, we set aside time to mark the seasons, celebrate the world's great traditions, and cultivate a joyful community spirit.

    Welcome Ceremony

    At the beginning of each year, the fifth grade welcomes incoming first graders in a beautiful ceremony. Each older child is paired with a younger child and offers a rose. At the end of the year this gesture of kindness is returned, as each first grader gives his or her fifth-grade buddy a rose to celebrate their friendship and the milestone of graduation

    Build a bonfire

    Lighting a campfire sparks warm memories for children with proper guidance, learning how to build a campfire and collection of woods be a great lesson for kids in the areas fire respect and cautio.

    Lantern Walk

    As the days grow short and dark, the bleakness of the season is held by the light of lanterns that the children make in class. The lanterns shed their glowing light as the early childhood classes walk together through the field and conservation land with parents and teachers.

    Middle School Arts Evening

    Held twice a year, this much-anticipated event gives students a chance to display their hard work in instrumental music, chorus, and eurythmy. For parents it is an affirmation of the rich and well-rounded education students receive at TS.

    May Day

    May Day is a traditional celebration of the arrival of spring and the return of the sun and flowers. On this day, all students, with their families and class teachers, gather to dance, sing seasonal songs. Strawberry shortcake is served, and families enjoy a picnic lunch.

    5th Grade Pentathlon

    This event recreates the original Olympic pentathlon, including the five signature events: long jump, javelin, swimming, wrestling, and 20 metre a distance run. Fifth grade students train throughout the year for the event.
